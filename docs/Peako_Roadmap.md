# Peako — Product Roadmap

*Rough, directional, subject to change. The goal of this doc is to give anyone joining the project (designer, dev, advisor) a mental map of where Peako is going — not to commit to dates.*

Last updated: April 2026

---

## TL;DR

| Phase | Name | What it proves | Status |
|-------|------|----------------|--------|
| 0 | **Prototype** | The "character commentary" concept is fun | ✅ Done (the current code) |
| 0.5 | **Internal Demo** | The full product day-in-the-life is convincing in a live pitch on a phone | ⏳ **In flight (next 4–6 weeks)** |
| 1 | **MVP / Alpha** | A real person opens the app daily for Peako's 3 challenges, and tells Peako what they ate | ⏸ Gated on Phase 0.5 buy-in |

| 3 | **Launch / Growth** | Peako retains users past week 2 | 🔮 |
| 4 | **Expansion** | Peako becomes a platform, not an app | 🪐 Speculative |

### The product shift (April 2026)

The roadmap was originally built around a **log-only** product — user logs food, Peako reacts. It's since evolved into a **coach + observer** product:

- **Primary loop:** Peako assigns 3 daily challenges → user does them → Peako reacts.
- **Secondary loop:** User logs meals → Peako reacts.

This makes Phase 1 meaningfully bigger (we now need a challenge engine + game library), but makes the product significantly stickier (Peako has a reason to push notifications and initiate contact). See `Peako_App_Structure.md` for the structural implications and `Peako_Games.md` for the placeholder game library.

### Market & growth model

**Target market:** overseas — US / UK / Canada / Australia / EU. English-first.

**Growth model:** the app is effectively **a printing press for shareable content.** Every day, Peako produces screenshot-worthy moments; users share those to Instagram Stories, TikTok, iMessage, X, Reddit. Organic spread is the primary user acquisition channel — not paid.

**Strategic consequence:** "viral-first" design moves from Phase 2 into **Phase 1 as a design constraint**. We don't need to build the full share infrastructure in Phase 1, but every screen must be designed *as if it might be screenshotted*. See `Peako_Design_Brief.md` §2 and §9 for the platform specs and the Screenshot Test.

The evaluation standard for a feature shifts from *"is it useful?"* to *"is it share-worthy?"*. If a moment in the app doesn't make the user want to show someone, it's probably not pulling its weight.

## Phase 0 — Prototype (now)

**Goal:** Prove the core loop is fun in a 60-second demo.

**What exists:**
- Web-based iPhone 15 shell (393 × 852px)
- One screen: **Peako's Feed**
- Three hardcoded log buttons: Salad, Pizza, Gym
- Three hardcoded Peako responses (one per log type)
- Stats header (streak + coins) — visual only, not functional

**What it doesn't do yet:**
- No real logging. No photos, no text input.
- No actual AI — responses are hardcoded strings.
- No accounts, no persistence, no backend.
- No other screens.

**Why this phase matters:**
This is the "does this make people smile?" test. Before investing in infrastructure, we need to know the *character* works. If the tone, voice, and visual identity land here, everything else is executable.

**Design needs this phase:** mascot concept, voice-of-brand, one hero screen (the feed). Phase 1 of the design brief.

## Phase 0.5 — Internal Demo (added 2026-04-23)

**Goal:** A single-device PWA that convincingly runs a full day-in-the-life of Peako, sufficient to pitch internally ("this is what we're building") and get buy-in to fund Phase 1. **Not for real users.**

This phase exists because the founder's boss translated "investment-ready MVP" to "investment-pitch-ready demo" — see [[Decisions/ADR-006-Internal-Demo-Scope]]. The full alpha ambitions of the original Phase 1 have been deferred until the internal pitch lands.

**What's in:**
- All three primary surfaces (Today, Progress, You) live enough to click through.
- Feed with real Peako posts generated by **Claude Haiku** (Anthropic), not hardcoded strings. Each demo produces different content.
- Full log flow (Meal with Healthy/Treat/Whatever tags + Other free text) → real LLM reaction appears in feed.
- All three challenge archetypes (Timer / Counter / Guided) with one playable example each.
- Swap + Skip flows working end-to-end (Peako auto re-rolls same archetype on swap).
- Streak, coins, streak-freeze mechanic — all tracked in localStorage, persisted across reloads.
- Onboarding (3 screens: Meet Peako → Roast+Difficulty → Notif permission + first-challenge preview).
- **Growth-system teaser:** Progress tab has the dual-view toggle (Trail / Dashboard), with a static placeholder Trail illustration (2–3 fake nodes) and the full Dashboard view. Debug menu in You tab can fire a Seed → Sprout transition and the Graduation Takeover on demand, for pitch variety. Real effort-day accounting + stage-specific mascot art land in Phase 1. Rationale in [[Decisions/ADR-008-Peako-Growth-System-Dual-Progress-Views]].
- Deployed to a public Vercel URL so the pitcher can open it on any iPhone as a PWA ("Add to Home Screen").
- Claude Haiku API calls proxied through one Vercel serverless function — key never ships to the client.

**What's explicitly out (and why):**
- **No accounts / no backend.** Single-device localStorage. If you clear Safari data, Peako forgets you. That's fine — nobody sharing this demo will care.
- **No push notifications.** Morning challenge drop is faked by just opening the app.
- **No analytics / no telemetry.** `console.log` is acceptable during build. No PostHog, no Mixpanel.
- **No photo upload for meal logs** in v1 of the demo. Tag picker + optional text caption is enough for the Peako reaction. Re-evaluate if the demo audience specifically asks to see photo handling.
- **No share / export flows.** The viral layer is Phase 2 and nothing about sharing needs to work for an internal pitch.
- **No rest-day scheduler** running automatically. A debug toggle in You tab lets the pitcher simulate a rest day on demand.
- **No TestFlight, no App Store, no Capacitor wrap.** The PWA "Add to Home Screen" experience is acceptable for the demo.

**Success criteria:**
1. Demoable on a real iPhone for 60–90 seconds without any screen that looks broken or placeholder-y.
2. The three signature moments land: (a) a challenge completion with Peako's in-voice reaction, (b) a pizza log that returns a screenshot-worthy roast, (c) the streak + coin + freeze mechanic is legible.
3. Internal audience says "OK, I get it" (or "OK, fund it") — not "wait, what are we looking at?"

**What happens after Phase 0.5 lands:**
- If internal buy-in → original Phase 1 kicks in: accounts, Supabase, push, analytics, mascot-locked design pass, 30-user alpha.
- If not → we learn something important and iterate the demo, or pivot.

**This phase is a one-shot.** Deliberately no fancy DX, no tests beyond smoke, no scaling thinking. The only thing that matters is that a human holding a phone is convinced.

## Phase 1 — MVP / Alpha *(gated on Phase 0.5 buy-in)*

**Goal:** A handful of real users open the app each day, complete Peako's 3 assigned challenges, optionally log a meal, and come back tomorrow.

**Screens needed** (full list in `Peako_App_Structure.md`):
1. **Onboarding** — Meet Peako. Set goals. Pick roast level + challenge difficulty.
2. **Today** (home) — 3 daily challenges, swap/skip controls, rest-day state, completion state.
3. **Challenge / Game modals** — timer, counter, guided-flow archetypes.
4. **Peako's Feed** (accessed via "phone" icon) — Peako's posts, pull-to-refresh.
5. **Log modal** — Meal + Other categories.
6. **Progress** — streak calendar, weekly summary, coins, lifetime stats.
7. **You** — profile, roast level, challenge difficulty, rest-day preference, settings.
8. **Empty + loading states** — high-character moments.

**Systems needed:**
- **Challenge engine** — daily assignment from game library, swap/skip logic (3 swaps/day, limited monthly skips), rest-day scheduler.
- **Game framework** — shared components for timer / counter / guided-flow game types; ~10 placeholder games.
- **LLM pipeline** — Peako's voice for challenges (intro, mid-game, completion, skip, rest-day, log reactions, weekly recap).
- Basic accounts + persistence.
- Streak + coin economy (including swap/skip costs).
- Image upload / handling.
- Push notifications (challenge drop in morning, feed-update notifications).

**Key design deliverables:**
- The mascot, locked and production-ready, with **coaching expressions** (demonstrating, counting, cheering-grudgingly, disappointed-you-quit) **and 4 growth-stage silhouettes** (Seed / Sprout / Teen / Fully Grown) — see [[Decisions/ADR-008-Peako-Growth-System-Dual-Progress-Views]].
- UI kit (colors, type, components).
- Full screen designs for all 8 screen groups above, **plus Progress — Trail view, Progress — Dashboard view, and the Graduation Takeover**.
- The **"phone" icon** — signature UI element for feed access.
- The **Trail biome illustration** — environment + 4 node markers + Peako sprite positioning.
- Challenge/game UI templates (one per archetype).
- Expression sheet for Peako — **stage × expression matrix**: ~6 expressions at Seed, ~10 at Sprout, full ~17 at Teen and Fully Grown. Includes two post-graduation registers (maintaining, worried) that unlock at Fully Grown.

**Success metric:** 30+ alpha users complete at least 2 of 3 daily challenges on 5 consecutive days.

**Known unknowns flagged for alpha testing:**
- Is "3 challenges" the right daily dose, or too much / too little?
- Do users actually use the log feature if it's secondary? Or does it wither?
- Does the phone-icon metaphor land, or do users miss the feed?
- Game UIs — which archetypes (timer vs counter vs guided) get completed vs abandoned?

## Phase 2 — Beta

**Goal:** Users *share* Peako's posts outside the app. The product becomes its own marketing channel.

**Features:**
- **Shareable post export** — one-tap screenshot/image generation of a Peako post, branded and ready for Instagram/TikTok/iMessage.
- **Sticker / reaction pack** — downloadable Peako stickers for iMessage, WhatsApp. This is a major viral lever.
- **Achievements & badges** — illustrated milestones with Peako-flavored names. Draft list (from the tagged-meal economy + challenge system):
  - *Reluctantly Proud* — 7 Healthy meals in a week (unlocks a rare Peako approval sticker)
  - *Full Confession* — 10 Treat meals logged (rewards honesty; unlocks "Peako sighing" sticker)
  - *Don't Hide From Me* — logged ≥1 meal every day for 14 days (any tag — unlocks "detective Peako" sticker)
  - *Balanced* — a week with ≥5 Healthy AND ≥1 Treat (rewards realistic behavior, not extreme dieting — protects against the "triggering content" line from Design Brief §9)
  - *First Week Survivor* — 7-day challenge streak
  - *Iron Plank* — longest plank time PR
  - *Swap King* — used all 3 swaps in a single day (honest effort at shaping your day)
- **Push notifications** — Peako "checking in" on you. Tone matters more than frequency.
- **Weekly recap card** — Sunday night, one shareable image summarizing your week through Peako's eyes (challenges done, meals logged, Healthy/Treat breakdown).

**Key design deliverables:**
- Share-card template system.
- 12–20 stickers.
- 10–15 achievement illustrations.
- Push notification copy + visual system.
- Weekly recap layout.

**Success metric:** at least 1 in 5 active users shares a Peako post externally per week.

## Phase 3 — Launch / Growth

**Goal:** Retention past week 2. Peako becomes a habit, not a novelty.

**Features being considered (not committed):**
- **Native iOS app** — shed the web shell.
- **Customization** — Peako outfits, moods, voice modes. Unlockable via streak.
- **Paid tier** — premium roast personalities (celebrity impressions? historical figures?), premium stickers, advanced stats.
- **Social layer (maybe)** — see your friend's Peako posts in a shared feed. Risky; could dilute the 1:1 intimacy. Will test carefully.

**Key design deliverables:** TBD based on what ships.

**Success metric:** Day-30 retention > 20% (industry benchmark for consumer health apps is ~5%).

## Phase 4 — Expansion (speculative)

**Questions we'd answer here, not decisions we've made:**
- Does the "Peako commentary" model work outside fitness? Study habits? Finances? Sleep?
- Is there a "Peako API" — a platform where other apps can add a snarky AI character?
- Android.

This phase is here to remind us that the real asset is **the character and the commentary engine**, not the fitness app specifically. Design decisions made now (especially around the mascot IP) should be made with the understanding that Peako might outlive any single vertical.

## What This Means for Design Work Right Now

We are executing **Phase 0.5 (Internal Demo)**. Concretely:

- **Do the mascot work now.** The character is the foundation for every phase. Phase 1 needs a *judging* register (for log reactions), a *coaching* register (for challenges), **and the 4 growth stages (Seed / Sprout / Teen / Fully Grown) plus post-graduation registers (maintaining, worried)**. Design all five registers with the same character's voice and all four stages with the same character's silhouette.
- **Phase 1 hero frames now cover ~8–10 screens**, not 1–2. See `Peako_App_Structure.md` for the list. The **Today screen** (with 3 challenges) is the most important front door; the **Graduation Takeover** is the most important share surface.
- **Design the "phone" icon as a first-class UI element.** It's the signature metaphor for the feed.
- **Design the Trail biome** as a standalone deliverable. It's the pre-graduation home of the Progress tab and a key source of "this is my journey" emotion.
- **Keep stickers & shareables in mind even now.** Every expression sketched for the mascot is a potential sticker later. The Graduation Takeover is a prime share-card candidate for Phase 2.
- **Design the system, not just the skin.** Colors, type, components — things that will survive into Phase 2 and 3.

See `Peako_Design_Brief.md` for the detailed Phase 1 ask.

## What We're Deliberately Not Doing

Useful to name out loud, because ambiguity leads to scope creep:

- **No calorie counting. No macros. No weight tracking.** Peako is a *vibes* app, not a data app. If users want numbers, they can use MyFitnessPal.
- **No meal plans.** Peako observes what you eat. They don't prescribe what to eat. *(Note: we DO prescribe workouts via challenges — that's the coaching half of the product. Food is the observation half.)*
- **No browsable workout library.** Peako assigns; the user doesn't shop for exercises. This is a feature, not a limitation — it forces Peako's agency in the relationship.
- **No custom workout scheduling.** Peako plans your week and your rest days.
- **No community / social network.** At least not in Phase 1–2. The intimacy of 1:1 with Peako is the product.
- **No Apple Watch / wearable integration** until Phase 3 at earliest.
